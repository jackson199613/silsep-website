# AquaPure™ Vietnam Rainy Season Feed Storage Challenge Report
## Silsep Technical Brief | 2026

---

### Executive Summary

Vietnam's aquaculture sector produces over 9 million tons of seafood annually, with the Mekong Delta accounting for more than 70% of shrimp output. During the rainy season (May–November), ambient humidity in the region regularly exceeds 85%, creating a critical but underaddressed challenge: **feed degradation through moisture absorption and anti-caking failure**.

This brief outlines the problem, its financial impact, and how AquaPure™ silica addresses it at the molecular level.

---

### The Problem: Humidity-Driven Feed Loss

**What happens to feed in 85%+ humidity:**

1. **Caking & Bridging** — Hygroscopic ingredients (vitamins, amino acids, enzyme additives) absorb moisture and fuse into solid masses. Feed becomes non-flowable, blocking automatic feeders.
2. **Enzyme Deactivation** — Moisture accelerates enzyme degradation before pellets reach the shrimp. Enzymes added at the mixing stage lose efficacy during storage.
3. **Mold & Mycotoxin Risk** — Water activity above 0.65 Aw enables mold growth within 48–72 hours in unsealed bags.
4. **FCR Deterioration** — Degraded feed requires higher quantities to achieve the same growth rate, directly inflating production costs.

**Estimated financial impact:**
- A mid-scale shrimp farm (500 tons/year feed consumption) can lose **5–8% of feed value** to moisture-related degradation during wet season months
- At USD $600/ton feed cost, that equals **$15,000–$24,000 in annual losses per operation**

### The AquaPure™ Solution

AquaPure™ is a food-grade amorphous silica (SiO₂) anti-caking agent engineered for high-humidity tropical aquaculture conditions.

**Mechanism of action:**
- Mesoporous silica structure provides a surface area of 300–600 m²/g
- Preferentially adsorbs moisture before hygroscopic feed ingredients, acting as a molecular buffer
- Inert at all pH levels — zero chemical interaction with vitamins, enzymes, or amino acids

**Field-validated performance (internal trials, Southeast Asia):**

| Parameter | Control (No Additive) | AquaPure™ at 0.5% |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Feed flowability at 85% RH | Failed at 4 hrs | Maintained at 24 hrs |
| Enzyme recovery at 95°C pelletizing | 40–45% | **88%** (Trial #22) |
| NH₃-N reduction in pond water | Baseline | **-1.5 ppm in 4 hrs** (Trial #31) |
| Feed-induced mortality risk | Baseline | Reduced significantly |
| FCR improvement | Baseline | 0.17 reduction observed |

### Application Guidelines

**Recommended dosage:** 0.3–0.5% of total feed formula weight

**Integration:** Direct addition at the mixing stage — compatible with twin-screw pelleting at temperatures up to 95°C

**Storage:** Standard 25kg moisture-barrier bags. No refrigeration required.

**Regulatory status:** Food-grade, inert — compliant with Vietnam MARD guidelines for feed additives. Zero residue at recommended dosage levels.

May–November represents the highest-risk period for feed degradation AND the highest production period for Vietnamese shrimp farmers targeting the year-end export peak.

Farms that stabilize feed quality during this window:

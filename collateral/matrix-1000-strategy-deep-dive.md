# Matrix 1000: Task Decomposition & Strategy (SILSEP)

To achieve the goal of **1,000 emails/day** with high conversion, we are breaking the execution into two core performance modules.

## Module 1: Source Quality (The "Foundation")
*Goal: Ensure 99%+ deliverability and 100% lead relevance.*

### 1.1 Deliverability (Technical Health)
- **Warm-up Protocol**: Monitor the 6 accounts on Instantly.ai. Ensure a gradual ramp-up (Day 1-14) to reach 50 emails/day per account.
- **Reputation Monitoring**: Daily check of SPF/DKIM/DMARC status and IP blacklists for `aqua-silsep.com`, `silsep-vn.com`, and `silsep-mekong.com`.
- **Inbox Placement Tests**: Weekly "Seed List" testing to ensure emails land in 'Primary' instead of 'Promotions' or 'Spam'.

### 1.2 Lead Quality (Targeting Precision)
- **Tiered Classification**:
    *   **Tier 1 (The Giants)**: C.P., Mavin, Dabaco, Skretting (Manual research + deep personalization).
    *   **Tier 2 (Regional Premix Plants)**: 500+ identified plants in Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia (Semi-automated sourcing).
- **Email Verification**: Use "NeverBounce" or similar logic to scrub the list. Zero tolerance for high bounce rates (which kill domain reputation).

---

## Module 2: Content Quality (The "Hook")
*Goal: Achieve 70%+ Open Rate and 5%+ Positive Response Rate.*

### 2.1 Opening Mastery (Subject Lines)
- **Subject A (Technical/Direct)**: "Technical Brief: FCR Optimization in Tropical Systems"
- **Subject B (Curiosity/Personal)**: "Question about {{company_name}}'s sustainable feed goals"
- **Subject C (Outcome-Focused)**: "12% FCR Reduction Case Study: Soc Trang Trials"
- **A/B Testing**: Run Subject A vs B for the first 100 leads to determine the winner.

### 2.2 Conversion Content (The Spintax Strategy)
- **Spintax Implementation**: Instead of one static template, we use variables like `{Hi|Hello|Dear} {{first_name}}, {I've been following|I noticed} your recent...` to ensure Google never sees the same email twice.
- **Value Proposition Logic**:
    *   **Hook**: Mention a specific local challenge (e.g., heat during the rainy season).
    *   **Evidence**: Cite the "Mekong Delta 12% FCR" trial.
    *   **CTA (Call to Action)**: Low-friction. "Open to a 5-minute technical brief?" (Avoid asking for a meeting in the first email).

### 2.3 The "Rule of 7" Follow-up Sequence
1. **Day 1**: Initial Value Prop.
2. **Day 3**: Technical Case Study (The Climate-Resilient Feed Brief).
3. **Day 7**: Social Proof (Testimonial/Trial data).
4. **Day 14**: The "Break-up" or "Re-engagement" (Short & Direct).
